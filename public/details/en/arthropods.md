# Arthropods (*Arthropoda*)

## Overview
Arthropods form the most species-rich animal phylum: **over one million described species**, or ~80% of all known animal species. They are distinguished by a jointed exoskeleton and paired, articulated appendages.

## Common characteristics
- **Exoskeleton** made of chitin (must moult to grow)
- Segmented body
- Paired, jointed appendages
- Ganglionated nervous system

## Major classes
| Class | Legs | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Insecta | 6 | Bee, mosquito, beetle |
| Arachnida | 8 | Spider, scorpion, mite |
| Crustacea | > 8 | Crab, lobster, shrimp |
| Myriapoda | > 30 | Centipede, millipede |

## Evolutionary success
Their protective exoskeleton, small size and adaptability explain their dominance in almost every terrestrial and aquatic ecosystem on Earth.
