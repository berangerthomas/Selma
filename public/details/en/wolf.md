# Gray Wolf (*Canis lupus*)

## Overview
The gray wolf is the largest wild canid and the ancestor of the domestic dog (*Canis lupus familiaris*). It lives in hierarchical packs across a vast range spanning Eurasia and North America.

## Social organisation
Packs are led by a dominant (alpha) breeding pair and typically number 5 to 15 individuals. Communication relies on **howling**, body postures and scent marking.

## Diet
The wolf is a large-game hunter targeting deer, elk, caribou and bison. By regulating ungulate populations, it acts as a **keystone species** in its ecosystem.

## Territory
Each pack defends a territory of 80 to 2,000 km² depending on prey availability.

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** globally (IUCN), but locally threatened in many regions. Following natural recolonisation from Italy in the 1990s, France's wolf population exceeded 1,000 individuals in 2024.
