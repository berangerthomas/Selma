# Polar Bear (*Ursus maritimus*)

## Overview
The polar bear is the world's largest terrestrial carnivore and a symbol of the Arctic. Classified among marine carnivores, it spends most of its life on sea ice and is a strong long-distance swimmer.

## Adaptations to the Arctic
- Translucent (appearing white) fur that traps solar heat
- Black skin beneath, absorbing UV radiation
- Up to 10 cm of subcutaneous fat for insulation
- Large paddle-like paws for swimming

## Diet
Feeds primarily on **ringed seals**, ambushing them at breathing holes in the ice. An adult can consume 30 kg of blubber in a single meal.

## Reproduction
Females give birth to 1–3 cubs in a snow den during winter. Cubs remain with their mother for ~2.5 years.

## Conservation status
**Vulnerable** (IUCN). Accelerating sea-ice loss due to climate change is severely reducing its habitat and hunting opportunities. Global population estimated at 20,000–31,000 individuals.
