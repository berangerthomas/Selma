# Old Uyghur Script

## Overview
The Old Uyghur script was developed by the **Uyghur Khaganate** (744–840 CE) based on the Sogdian alphabet. It represents a pivotal step in the transmission of writing across Central Asia.

## Direction Change
Remarkably, Old Uyghur script rotated the direction of writing: while Sogdian was written right to left horizontally, the Uyghurs rotated the text **90°** and wrote it **top to bottom, left to right** in vertical columns — a convention inherited by Mongolian and Manchu scripts.

## Uses
The Uyghur script served as the literary and administrative vehicle for the Uyghur Empire and its successor states. It was also used to write:
- Buddhist, Manichaean and Nestorian Christian religious texts
- Administrative documents
- Translations of Sanskrit, Chinese and Sogdian texts

## Legacy
When **Genghis Khan** unified the Mongol tribes in 1206, he commissioned a Uyghur scribe to adapt the Uyghur script for the Mongolian language — giving rise to the **traditional Mongolian script** still officially used in Inner Mongolia (China) today.
