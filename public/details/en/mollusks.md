# Mollusks (*Mollusca*)

## Overview
Mollusks form the second most species-rich animal phylum, with approximately **85,000 described species** inhabiting marine, freshwater and terrestrial environments.

## Body plan
Despite remarkable diversity, mollusks share core features:
- A muscular **foot** (locomotion, anchoring)
- A **mantle** that often secretes a calcareous shell
- A **radula** (rasping tongue, absent in bivalves)
- **Gills** or lungs depending on habitat

## Major classes
| Class | Examples | Features |
|---|---|---|
| Gastropods | Snails, slugs, cones | Spiralled or absent shell |
| Bivalves | Mussels, oysters, clams | Two valves, filter feeding |
| Cephalopods | Octopuses, squid, nautilus | Highly intelligent, no external shell |
| Polyplacophorans | Chitons | Eight articulated plates |

## Human significance
- Food: oysters, mussels, squid, snails, abalone
- Industry: mother-of-pearl (nacre), byssus silk
- Medicine: conotoxins from cone snails used to develop novel analgesics
- Ecology: bivalves filter vast volumes of water, improving water clarity
