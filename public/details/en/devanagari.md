# Devanagari

## Overview
Devanagari (*"divine city script"*) is the most widely used script of the Brahmi family, serving as the writing system for **Hindi, Sanskrit, Marathi, Nepali** and numerous other South Asian languages.

## Structure
Devanagari is an **abugida**: each of its 47 primary characters (33 consonants + 14 vowels) carries an inherent /a/ vowel. The characteristic **horizontal bar** (*shirodha*) running along the top of letters and connecting them horizontally is unique to Devanagari and its closest relatives.

## Key Features
- 13 independent vowel characters
- 33 consonant characters, each with built-in /a/
- Vowel diacritics above, below or around consonants to change the vowel
- A special mark (*halant/virama*) to suppress the inherent vowel
- Written **left to right**

## Languages and Speakers
Devanagari is used by approximately **600 million** people:
- **Hindi** — 600 million speakers (official language of India)
- **Sanskrit** — classical sacred language
- **Marathi** — Maharashtra state (~80 million speakers)
- **Nepali** — official language of Nepal

## Digital Adoption
Devanagari is fully supported in Unicode and is one of the most-used scripts on the internet, reflecting India's growing digital presence.
