# Squamata

## Overview
Squamata is the largest reptile order with approximately **10,000 species**, comprising lizards (~6,500 sp.), snakes (~3,700 sp.) and amphisbaenians (worm lizards).

## Defining feature
Skin covered with overlapping keratinous scales. Squamates shed their skin (ecdysis) periodically to grow and remove parasites.

## Lizards
Highly diverse morphology: well-developed limbs (absent in legless lizards), often autotomisable tail. Bifid tongue used to sample chemical cues (Jacobson's organ).

## Snakes
Derived from burrowing lizard ancestors that lost their limbs. Elongated body, venom-injecting fangs (in venomous species), extensible jaw enabling ingestion of large prey.

## Diet diversity
Insectivorous, carnivorous, herbivorous (rare in snakes). Monitors and pythons can swallow prey much larger than their own head.
