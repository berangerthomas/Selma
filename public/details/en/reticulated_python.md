# Reticulated Python (*Malayopython reticulatus*)

## Overview
The reticulated python is the **world's longest snake**. Specimens exceeding **7 metres** are regularly recorded; the documented record stands at 7.67 m.

## Habitat
Humid tropical forests, forest edges, agricultural land and urban fringes across South-East Asia (Indonesia, Philippines, Myanmar, Malaysia).

## Feeding
A non-venomous constrictor. It seizes prey (mammals, birds, reptiles) with its teeth and kills by constriction. It can swallow deer, wild pigs and, exceptionally, humans.

## Reproduction
Oviparous: the female lays 15–100 eggs and broods them by muscular contractions that generate metabolic heat.

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** (IUCN). Hunted for its skin (leather industry) and taken for the pet trade. An invasive species in Florida, where released individuals are colonising the Everglades ecosystem.
