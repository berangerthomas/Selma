# Invertebrates

## Definition
Invertebrates are animals lacking a vertebral column. This heterogeneous grouping represents **over 95%** of all known animal species (~1.3 million out of ~1.5 million described).

## Major phyla
| Phylum | Examples | Approx. species |
|---|---|---|
| Arthropoda | Insects, spiders, crustaceans | > 1,000,000 |
| Mollusca | Snails, octopuses, mussels | ~85,000 |
| Annelida | Earthworms, leeches | ~22,000 |
| Echinodermata | Starfish, sea urchins | ~7,000 |
| Cnidaria | Jellyfish, corals, anemones | ~11,000 |
| Porifera | Sponges | ~10,000 |

## Ecological and economic importance
- Pollination (bees, butterflies)
- Decomposition of organic matter
- Base of marine food chains (zooplankton)
- Water filtration (oysters, mussels, sponges)
- Human food source (mollusks, crustaceans)
