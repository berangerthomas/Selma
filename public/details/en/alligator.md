# American Alligator (*Alligator mississippiensis*)

## Overview
The American alligator is one of only two surviving alligator species (alongside the Chinese alligator). It is iconic in the wetlands of the south-eastern United States.

## Difference from crocodiles
The alligator has a **wide, rounded (U-shaped) snout**, while crocodiles have a narrow, pointed (V-shaped) snout. When an alligator closes its mouth, only the upper teeth are visible.

## Habitat
Marshes, bayous, slow-moving rivers and lakes in Florida, Louisiana and surrounding states. Digs burrows and "gator holes" that serve as vital refuges for other wetland species.

## Temperature regulation
Basks in the sun to warm up; retreats to shade or water to cool down. Enters torpor (brumation) during cold winters.

## Conservation — a remarkable success story
Listed as **Endangered** in the 1960s after intensive hide hunting. Following strict protection, the population rebounded to **~5 million** individuals in the United States. Removed from the endangered list in 1987.
