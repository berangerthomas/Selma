# Common Bottlenose Dolphin (*Tursiops truncatus*)

## Overview
The bottlenose dolphin is one of the most studied cetaceans. Highly adaptable, it inhabits virtually all temperate and tropical oceans worldwide.

## Intelligence and cognition
- Recognises its own reflection in a mirror
- Uses marine sponges as tools to probe the seafloor
- Cooperates in sophisticated hunting strategies
- Long-term social memory

## Communication
Each individual has a unique **signature whistle**, functioning as a name. Groups emit echolocation clicks to detect and track prey.

## Social structure
Lives in groups of 10 to 30 individuals (occasionally hundreds). Associations can last for decades.

## Interactions with humans
An iconic species in cognitive research. Coastal populations in some areas spontaneously cooperate with artisanal fishers (e.g. Laguna, Brazil).

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** globally (IUCN), though some coastal populations are threatened by pollution, bycatch and habitat degradation.
