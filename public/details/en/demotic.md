# Demotic Script

## Overview
Demotic ("popular script" in Greek) was the everyday writing system of ancient Egypt from around **650 BCE** to the 5th century CE. It is far more abbreviated than either hieroglyphs or hieratic, and represented a significant shift toward pure phonographic writing.

## Development
Demotic evolved from the Northern Hieratic tradition and became the dominant script under the **Late Period** and **Ptolemaic** pharaohs. It was used for:
- Legal and administrative documents
- Commercial records
- Literature and scientific texts
- Religious inscriptions

## The Rosetta Stone Connection
The Rosetta Stone carries its central panel in Demotic — the language of the priests and administration at the time (196 BCE). This panel was crucial in Champollion's decipherment, as it bridged hieroglyphs and Greek.

## Legacy
Demotic contributed the sign inventory that, combined with the Greek alphabet, produced **Coptic** — the final stage of the Egyptian language, still used liturgically in the Coptic Orthodox Church today.
