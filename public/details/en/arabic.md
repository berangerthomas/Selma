# Arabic Script

## Overview
The Arabic script descends from **Nabataean** (its primary structural ancestor) and **Syriac** (which contributed letter forms and the tradition of connecting letters in cursive). Today it is the second most widely used writing system in the world.

## Key Features
- An **abjad**: 28 consonantal letters; short vowels are optional diacritics (*harakat*)
- Written **right to left**
- Fully **cursive**: letters connect in all positions (initial, medial, final, isolated)
- Letters change shape depending on their position in the word

## Calligraphic Traditions
Arabic calligraphy is considered one of the highest art forms in Islamic culture. Major styles include:

| Style | Character |
|---|---|
| **Kufic** | Angular, early style used in Quranic manuscripts |
| **Naskh** | Clear, rounded — basis for printed Arabic |
| **Thuluth** | Monumental, used in mosque decorations |
| **Nastaliq** | Elegant flowing style; dominant in Persian and Urdu |

## Geographic Reach
The Arabic script is used to write Arabic, Persian, Urdu, Pashto, Uyghur (historically), Malay (Jawi) and many other languages — serving over **1.8 billion** people.
