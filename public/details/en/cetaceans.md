# Cetaceans

## Definition
Cetaceans (*Cetacea*) are fully aquatic placental mammals descended from terrestrial artiodactyl ancestors some 50 million years ago. About 90 species are known.

## Two suborders
- **Mysticetes** (baleen whales): filter-feed on plankton through keratin plates (baleen). E.g. blue whale, fin whale.
- **Odontocetes** (toothed whales): active hunters capable of echolocation. E.g. dolphins, sperm whale, orca.

## Marine adaptations
- Streamlined, fusiform body with forelimbs modified as flippers
- No external hind limbs
- Breathing through one or two blowholes (dorsal nostrils)
- Highly developed brain, especially in odontocetes

## Communication
Cetaceans communicate via clicks, whistles and complex songs (humpback whale). Bottlenose dolphins have unique individual signature whistles.

## Conservation
Several species threatened by historical whaling, ship strikes, acoustic pollution and plastic ingestion.
