# Raptors

## Definition
Raptors (birds of prey) are carnivorous birds equipped with **powerful talons**, a **hooked beak** and acute eyesight. They are divided into diurnal raptors (Accipitriformes, Falconiformes) and nocturnal raptors (Strigiformes).

## Hunting adaptations
- Forward-facing eyes for binocular vision and accurate distance estimation
- Sharp talons for seizing and killing prey
- Curved beak for tearing flesh
- Exceptional hearing (especially owls)

## Notable diurnal raptors
Eagles, falcons, hawks, buzzards, vultures, kites.

## Nocturnal raptors
Owls: silent flight enabled by fringed feathers, facial disc that amplifies sounds.

## Ecological role
Regulators of rodent, rabbit and bird populations. Vultures act as indispensable scavengers, limiting the spread of disease.

## Conservation
Several species nearly disappeared due to direct persecution and pesticides (DDT). Many populations have recovered through dedicated protection programmes.
