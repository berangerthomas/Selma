# Latin Alphabet

## Overview
The Latin alphabet emerged in **Latium** (central Italy) around **700 BCE**, adapted from Etruscan with direct influence from the Western Greek alphabet. It became the script of the Roman Republic, the Roman Empire, and — through them — the world.

## Parents
Latin has two parents:
- **Etruscan**: the primary structural model (letter shapes, direction)
- **Greek**: direct influence (some letter forms, the concept of vowel letters)

## Classical Latin Alphabet
The classical Latin alphabet had **23 letters**:  
A B C D E F G H I K L M N O P Q R S T V X Y Z  
(No J, U or W — these were added in the Middle Ages and Renaissance.)

## Spread with Rome
Latin spread across Europe as the Roman Empire expanded. After Rome's fall, it remained the language of the Church, scholarship and government throughout medieval Europe.

## Romance Language Descendants
As spoken Latin evolved in different regions, it gave rise to the **Romance languages**:

| Language | Country | Speakers |
|---|---|---|
| Spanish | Spain, Latin America | ~500 million |
| Portuguese | Portugal, Brazil | ~250 million |
| French | France, Africa, Canada | ~280 million |
| Italian | Italy | ~65 million |
| Romanian | Romania | ~25 million |
| Catalan | Spain, France | ~10 million |

## Global Reach
Today, the **Modern Roman Alphabet** (derived from Latin) is used by over **3 billion people** across 100+ languages — more than any other writing system.
