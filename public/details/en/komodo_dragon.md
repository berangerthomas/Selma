# Komodo Dragon (*Varanus komodoensis*)

## Overview
The Komodo dragon is the world's largest living lizard. It is endemic to five Indonesian islands: Komodo, Rinca, Flores, Gili Motang and Padar.

## Dimensions
- Length: up to **3 metres**
- Weight: up to **70 kg**

## Hunting and venom
Once thought to kill only through bacteria in its saliva, the Komodo dragon is now known to possess **venom glands** that produce an anticoagulant. Bitten prey gradually weakens and bleeds out.

## Diet
An opportunistic carnivore: deer, goats, wild pigs, water buffalo, carrion. Can consume **80% of its own body weight** in a single meal.

## Reproduction
Females can reproduce **parthenogenetically** (without a male), producing exclusively male offspring.

## Conservation status
**Endangered** (IUCN). Wild population estimated at ~1,400–2,000 individuals. Protected within Komodo National Park (UNESCO World Heritage Site).
