# Nile Crocodile (*Crocodylus niloticus*)

## Overview
The Nile crocodile is Africa's largest reptile and the world's second-largest crocodilian. It inhabits rivers, lakes and deltas across sub-Saharan Africa and Madagascar.

## Dimensions
- Length: 4–6 m (adult males)
- Weight: 500–1,000 kg

## Hunting
A deadly ambush predator. Targets fish, antelopes, zebras, buffalo and occasionally humans. Uses the **"death roll"** technique to dismember prey.

## Reproduction
The female lays 40–80 eggs in a sandy nest. She guards it for ~90 days and carries hatchlings to the water at emergence.

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** (IUCN). Population stable, estimated at ~50,000–70,000 individuals, thanks to legal protections in place since the 1970s.
