# Passerines (*Passeriformes*)

## Overview
Passerines are the most diverse order of birds: **over 6,500 species**, accounting for ~60% of all avian species. They are commonly known as "perching birds" or "songbirds".

## Characteristics
- Anisodactyl foot (3 toes forward, 1 back) adapted for perching
- Highly developed vocal organ (syrinx) enabling complex song
- Generally small to medium body size

## Subgroups
- **Suboscines**: less vocal complexity (tyrant flycatchers, manakins)
- **Oscines** (true songbirds): the majority, with learnt song

## Ecological importance
Pollinators, seed dispersers, insect predators. Their abundance makes them key **bioindicators of ecosystem health**.

## Distribution
Present on all continents except Antarctica. Some species undertake transcontinental migrations.
