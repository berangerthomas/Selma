# Carnivores

## Definition
The order *Carnivora* comprises mammals whose diet is based primarily on animal flesh. They are characterised by **carnassial teeth** (modified premolars and molars) adapted for shearing meat.

## Main families
- **Felidae**: lion, tiger, cheetah, domestic cat
- **Canidae**: wolf, fox, coyote, dog
- **Ursidae**: grizzly, polar bear, giant panda
- **Mustelidae**: otter, mink, badger
- **Pinnipeds**: seal, walrus, sea lion

## Adaptations
- Forward-facing eyes for depth perception (essential for predation)
- Retractile claws (felids) or fixed claws
- Highly developed hearing and sense of smell
- Powerful jaws with specialised dentition

## Ecological role
Carnivores regulate prey populations, preventing overgrazing and maintaining ecosystem balance. The reintroduction of wolves to Yellowstone famously triggered a trophic cascade that reshaped the entire park.
