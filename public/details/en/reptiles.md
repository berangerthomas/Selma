# Reptiles

## Definition
Reptiles are ectothermic vertebrates with scaly or plated skin. This paraphyletic group traditionally includes lizards, snakes, turtles, crocodilians and the tuatara.

## Thermoregulation
Reptiles regulate body temperature through behaviour (basking, shade-seeking) rather than metabolism. This dramatically reduces their energy requirements compared with mammals and birds.

## Major orders
| Order | Examples | Species |
|---|---|---|
| Squamata | Snakes, lizards | ~10,000 |
| Testudines | Turtles | ~350 |
| Crocodilia | Crocodiles, alligators | ~25 |
| Rhynchocephalia | Tuatara | 1 |

## Relationship with birds
Crocodilians are more closely related to birds than to lizards — together they form the Archosauria.

## Conservation
More than one-fifth of reptile species are threatened, mainly due to the destruction of tropical habitats.
