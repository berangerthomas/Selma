# Giant Pacific Octopus (*Enteroctopus dofleini*)

## Overview
The giant Pacific octopus is the largest living cephalopod, inhabiting the cold coastal waters of the North Pacific from California to Alaska and across to Japan.

## Size
- Arm span: up to **6 metres**
- Weight: typically 15–50 kg; the largest verified individual weighed **272 kg**

## Intelligence
Widely regarded as the most intelligent invertebrate:
- Solves complex puzzles and opens jars
- Learns by observation
- Engages in spontaneous play (rare among invertebrates)
- Retains long-term memory

## Camouflage
Changes colour, texture and shape in milliseconds using **chromatophores** (pigment cells), **iridophores** (light-reflecting cells) and **papillae** (skin projections).

## Reproduction
Semelparous: the female lays **20,000 to 100,000 eggs**, guarding and aerating them without eating for 4–7 months before dying. The male dies shortly after mating.

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** (IUCN). Principal threats include commercial fishing bycatch and ocean warming affecting prey availability.
