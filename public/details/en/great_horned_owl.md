# Great Horned Owl (*Bubo virginianus*)

## Overview
The great horned owl is the largest nocturnal raptor in the Americas. It is unmistakable thanks to its prominent **ear tufts** (tufted feathers resembling horns) and its deep five-note call.

## Habitat
Remarkably adaptable: forests, deserts, grasslands, urban areas. One of the most widely distributed birds in the Americas, from Canada to Patagonia.

## Nocturnal hunting
- Vision adapted to low light (enlarged pupils)
- Asymmetrical ears for precise sound-location of prey in total darkness
- Silent flight thanks to fringed feathers
- Prey: rabbits, rodents, skunks, other owls, small raccoons

## Reproduction
Often nests in abandoned stick nests of other species (corvids, ospreys). Lays 1–4 eggs as early as January–February, well before winter ends.

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** (IUCN). Population stable and abundant.
