## Overview
The Greek alphabet, adapted from Phoenician around **800 BCE**, made a decisive innovation: it systematically encoded **both consonants and vowels**. This made it the first true **alphabet** in the strict sense. It is the ancestor of Latin, Cyrillic and several other major scripts.

## The Key Innovation
Phoenician had signs for consonants only. The Greeks repurposed several Phoenician signs for sounds absent in Greek as **vowel letters** (Alpha, Epsilon, Iota, Omicron, Upsilon). This seemingly small change had vast consequences for literacy and phonological analysis.

## Script Legacy
Greek is the most prolific script parent in European history:
- → **Etruscan** → **Latin** → virtually all European scripts
- → **Glagolitic** → **Cyrillic** (Slavic world)
- → **Coptic** (Egypt, with Demotic)
- → **Armenian** (with Syriac)
