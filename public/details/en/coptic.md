# Coptic Script

## Overview
Coptic is both the final stage of the **Egyptian language** (a direct descendant of the ancient language of the pharaohs) and a writing system that reflects a unique convergence: it uses **Greek letters** supplemented by signs from **Demotic** to represent Egyptian sounds absent in Greek.

## Historical Context
The Coptic script emerged between the 2nd and 4th centuries CE, as Greek-speaking Christians in Egypt needed to translate the **Bible** and early **Gnostic texts** into the Egyptian vernacular.

## Dialects
Five major literary dialects existed, of which **Sahidic** (Upper Egypt) and **Bohairic** (Lower Egypt) are the most important.

## Living Language
Coptic ceased to be a spoken language around the **12th–17th century CE** as Arabic became dominant. It survives today exclusively as the **liturgical language** of the **Coptic Orthodox Church**, used in weekly services by approximately 10 million Egyptian Christians.
