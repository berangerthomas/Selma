# Brahmi Script

## Overview
Brahmi is the foundational script of South and Southeast Asia, attested from the **3rd century BCE**. It is the ancestor of over **100 modern scripts** — the largest script family in the world by number of derived systems.

## Origins — A Scholarly Debate
The origin of Brahmi remains debated:
- The dominant theory holds that it was adapted from the **Aramaic** abjad (possibly via a now-lost intermediary)
- A minority view argues for independent invention, given significant structural differences from Semitic scripts
- A third view proposes derivation from an earlier Indus Valley script (unproven)

Most scholars accept an **Aramaic-based origin** with significant local innovation.

## The Brahmi Innovation: Abugida
Unlike its likely ancestor (Aramaic, an abjad), Brahmi is an **abugida**: each consonant symbol inherently carries a vowel /a/. Other vowels are represented by diacritical marks. This systematic vowel marking system was passed on to all its descendants.

## Ashoka's Rock Edicts
The oldest securely dated Brahmi inscriptions are the **Edicts of Ashoka** (c. 250 BCE), found across the Indian subcontinent. Emperor Ashoka used Brahmi to spread Buddhist teachings to his subjects.
