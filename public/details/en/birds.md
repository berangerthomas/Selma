# Birds

## Definition
Birds (*Aves*) are warm-blooded vertebrates covered in **feathers**, bearing a toothless keratin beak, forelimbs evolved as wings, and a lightweight skeleton. They are the only living descendants of theropod dinosaurs.

## Diversity
~10,500 species described, ranging from the bee hummingbird (2 g) to the ostrich (150 kg). They have colonised every continent, including Antarctica.

## Flight adaptations
- Hollow (pneumatised) bones to reduce mass
- Enlarged keel (sternum) for anchoring powerful pectoral muscles
- Contour feathers (aerodynamics) and down (insulation)
- High metabolic rate

## Reproduction
All birds are oviparous. Most build nests and incubate their eggs. Parental care ranges from a few days to several years.

## Senses
Exceptional vision (some species see UV light), magnetoreception for migratory navigation, complex song for communication and mate attraction.
