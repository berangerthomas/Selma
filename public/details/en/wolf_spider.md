# Wolf Spider (*Lycosidae*)

## Overview
Wolf spiders form the family *Lycosidae*, with over **2,400 species** found worldwide. Their common name reflects their hunting strategy: like wolves, they actively stalk and chase their prey.

## Physical features
- **Eight eyes** arranged in three rows; two large posterior-lateral eyes have a reflective tapetum, causing eyeshine.
- Robust, hairy body with long, powerful legs.
- Brown, grey and black cryptic colouring.

## Hunting behaviour
Wolf spiders do **not build a prey-capture web**. They hunt by sight and speed, either ambushing or running down insects, small invertebrates and occasionally small vertebrates.

## Maternal care
The female carries her egg sac attached to her spinnerets. After hatching, spiderlings ride on their mother's back for several weeks — exceptional parental investment for arachnids.

## Habitat
Found in virtually every terrestrial habitat: grasslands, forests, coastlines, gardens and even homes. Several species burrow into the ground.

## Danger to humans
Bites are generally painful but not medically significant in most species.
