# Hieratic Script

## Overview
Hieratic is the cursive form of Egyptian hieroglyphs, developed for writing on papyrus with a rush pen or brush. It was the primary administrative and religious script of ancient Egypt for over 2,000 years.

## Origins
Hieratic emerged almost simultaneously with hieroglyphs, around **2600 BCE**, as scribes needed a faster way to write. Each hieratic sign corresponds to a specific hieroglyph, but simplified beyond recognition.

## Uses
- **Religious texts**: the Book of the Dead, funerary literature
- **Administrative documents**: census records, legal contracts, tax lists
- **Literary works**: stories, wisdom texts, medical and mathematical papyri

## Notable Example
The **Rhind Mathematical Papyrus** (c. 1550 BCE), written in hieratic, contains 84 mathematical problems and is one of the oldest known mathematical documents.

## Transition to Demotic
From around 650 BCE, Hieratic was progressively replaced by **Demotic** for secular use, while retaining its role in religious contexts until the 3rd century CE.
