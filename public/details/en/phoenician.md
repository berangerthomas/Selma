# Phoenician Alphabet

![Phoenician to modern scripts correspondence](/assets/images/phoenician_table.svg)

## Overview
The Phoenician alphabet, developed around **1050 BCE** in the city-states of the Levantine coast (modern Lebanon), is one of the most consequential writing inventions in history. It is the direct ancestor of almost every alphabet used in the world today.

## Structure
Phoenician is a **pure abjad** — it writes only consonants (22 of them), leaving vowels to be inferred by the reader from context. It is written **right to left**.

## Key Innovation
The Phoenicians did not invent the alphabet — that honour goes to **Proto-Sinaitic**. But their maritime trade network carried the alphabet across the Mediterranean, making it the vector for global alphabetic literacy.
