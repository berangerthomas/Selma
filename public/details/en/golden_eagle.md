# Golden Eagle (*Aquila chrysaetos*)

## Overview
The golden eagle is one of the most widespread and emblematic raptors in the Northern Hemisphere. It nests in mountains, cliffs and open woodlands across Eurasia, North America and North Africa.

## Morphology
- Wingspan: 1.8–2.2 m
- Weight: 3–6.5 kg (females larger than males)
- Dark-brown plumage with golden-buff nape and crown

## Hunting
A swift aerial predator, it dives at over **300 km/h** (stoop). Targets hares, rabbits, marmots, foxes and occasionally young deer.

## Territory
A pair defends a territory of 50 to 200 km². The same eyrie (nest) is reused for decades and enlarged each year.

## Symbolism
National emblem of numerous countries (USA, Mexico, Albania…) and a widespread heraldic figure since Antiquity.

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** (IUCN). ~300,000 individuals worldwide. In France, ~500 breeding pairs inhabit the Alps, Pyrenees and Massif Central.
