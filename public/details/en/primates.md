# Primates

## Definition
The order *Primates* encompasses mammals characterised by a large brain, prehensile hands, forward-facing eyes (stereoscopic vision) and extended parental care.

## Suborders
- **Strepsirrhines**: lemurs, galagos, lorises — more ancestral, often nocturnal.
- **Haplorhines**: tarsiers, New World monkeys, Old World monkeys, hominoids (great apes + humans).

## Brain and intelligence
Primates have the highest brain-to-body mass ratio among mammals. Great apes use tools, solve abstract problems and show partial theory of mind.

## Distribution
Primarily tropical and subtropical (Africa, South-East Asia, Latin America). Humans are the sole species to have colonised every continent.

## Conservation
~60% of primate species are threatened with extinction, primarily due to deforestation and illegal trade.
