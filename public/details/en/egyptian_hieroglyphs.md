# Egyptian Hieroglyphs

## Overview
Egyptian hieroglyphs are one of the earliest writing systems ever devised, attested from around **3200 BCE**. The system combines logographic, syllabic and alphabetic elements — making it one of the most complex scripts in history.

## Structure
Hieroglyphs can function in three ways simultaneously:
- **Logograms** — a sign represents a whole word (e.g. 𓂀 = "eye")
- **Phonograms** — a sign represents one, two or three consonants
- **Determinatives** — silent signs that indicate the semantic category of a word

## Decipherment
Hieroglyphs fell out of use after the 4th century CE and remained undeciphered for 1,400 years. The **Rosetta Stone** (196 BCE), bearing the same priestly decree in hieroglyphs, Demotic and Greek, allowed **Jean-François Champollion** to crack the code in 1822.

## Script Family
Hieroglyphs gave rise to two cursive offshoots:
- **Hieratic** — a rapid priestly script used on papyrus from c. 2600 BCE
- **Demotic** — an even more cursive administrative script from c. 650 BCE

They also inspired **Proto-Sinaitic**, the alphabet that eventually became Phoenician and — through it — nearly every alphabet used today.

## Duration
Egyptian hieroglyphs were in active use for over **3,500 years**, from c. 3200 BCE to 394 CE, when the last known hieroglyphic inscription was carved at Philae.
