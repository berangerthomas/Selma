# Honeybee (*Apis mellifera*)

## Overview
The honeybee is the world's most important managed pollinator and one of the most studied insects. Colonies of **20,000 to 80,000 individuals** live in highly organised societies with a sophisticated division of labour.

## Colony structure
- **Queen**: the sole reproductive female, capable of laying up to 2,000 eggs per day.
- **Workers** (sterile females): foraging, nursing, building, guarding — all colony tasks.
- **Drones** (males): sole purpose is to mate with a new queen.

## Honey production
Worker bees collect **nectar** from flowers and transport it in a honey stomach to the hive. Enzymes and evaporation transform it into honey, sealed in wax cells for long-term storage.

## Pollination
A single colony may visit millions of flowers daily. Roughly **35% of global food production** depends directly or indirectly on insect pollinators, with honeybees playing a central role.

## Threats
Honeybees face mounting pressures: **neonicotinoid pesticides**, the *Varroa destructor* mite, fungal diseases, viral pathogens and loss of floral diversity all contribute to colony collapse disorder.
