# Insects (*Insecta*)

## Overview
Insects are the most species-rich animal class, with **over one million described species** and millions more awaiting discovery. They colonise virtually every terrestrial and freshwater environment.

## Defining morphology
- **3 pairs of legs** (6 legs total)
- Body divided into **3 tagmata**: head, thorax, abdomen
- 1 pair of antennae
- Usually 2 pairs of wings (sometimes one pair or none)

## Metamorphosis
- **Holometabolous** (complete): egg → larva → pupa → adult (butterflies, bees, beetles)
- **Hemimetabolous** (incomplete): egg → nymph → adult (grasshoppers, dragonflies)

## Ecological roles
- **Pollinators** (~75% of flowering plants depend on them)
- **Decomposers** (dung beetles, necrophagous species)
- **Prey** for birds, bats, reptiles…
- **Soil engineers** (ants, termites)

## Global decline
Insect populations have collapsed by 40–75% over recent decades in some regions, raising major concerns for ecosystem functioning.
