# Vertebrates

## Definition
Vertebrates (*Vertebrata*) are animals possessing a vertebral column — a stack of bony or cartilaginous vertebrae surrounding the spinal cord — and a skull protecting the brain.

## Common characteristics
- Internal skeleton (endoskeleton) of bone or cartilage
- Well-developed central nervous system
- Multi-chambered heart
- Bilateral symmetry

## The five major classes
| Class | Examples | Environment |
|---|---|---|
| Fish | Salmon, shark | Aquatic |
| Amphibians | Frog, salamander | Amphibious |
| Reptiles | Crocodile, snake | Terrestrial/aquatic |
| Birds | Eagle, penguin | Terrestrial/aerial |
| Mammals | Lion, dolphin | Terrestrial/aquatic |

## Key figures
Approximately **65,000 vertebrate species** are described, making up fewer than 5% of all animal species, yet representing the majority of terrestrial animal biomass.
