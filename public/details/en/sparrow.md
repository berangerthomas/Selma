# House Sparrow (*Passer domesticus*)

## Overview
The house sparrow is one of the most common and widespread birds on Earth, having coexisted with humans for ~10,000 years — since the advent of agriculture.

## Morphology
- Length: 14–16 cm, weight: 24–40 g
- Male: grey crown, black bib, streaked brown back
- Female and juvenile: duller, without bib

## Behaviour
- Lives in flocks, often noisy groups near buildings
- Nests in cavities: roof eaves, walls, nest boxes
- Diet: seeds, insects (especially during breeding season)

## Decline in Europe
Urban populations have fallen by **60% over 30 years** in many western European countries. Likely causes: loss of nesting sites, decline in invertebrates, pollution.

## Conservation status
**Least Concern** globally (IUCN), but a species of local concern in several European countries.
