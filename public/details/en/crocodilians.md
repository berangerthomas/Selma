# Crocodilians

## Overview
Crocodilians (*Crocodilia*) are among the oldest surviving vertebrates: their ancestors date back more than **200 million years**. The 25 living species are grouped in three families: Crocodylidae, Alligatoridae and Gavialidae.

## Morphology
- Dorsoventrally flattened body, elongated snout
- Among the most powerful bites in the animal kingdom
- Four short legs, powerful propulsive tail
- Eyes and nostrils positioned high on the head (ambush from waterline)

## Biology
- Ectothermic; most active at high temperatures
- Longevity: 70–100 years
- Elaborate parental care: females guard nests and carry hatchlings to water in their jaws

## Ecological role
They excavate waterholes ("gator holes") that serve as refuges for other species during dry seasons. They regulate fish and mammal populations in riparian ecosystems.
