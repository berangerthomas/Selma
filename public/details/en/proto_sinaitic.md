# Proto-Sinaitic Script

## Overview
Proto-Sinaitic is the earliest known alphabetic writing system, dating from around **1850–1400 BCE**. It was discovered primarily in the Sinai Peninsula, hence its name.

## Origins
The script was created by Semitic workers in Egypt who adapted a small set of Egyptian hieroglyphs as an alphabet. The principle — **acrophony** — assigned each sign the sound of the first consonant of its name in the Semitic language:
- The sign for *aleph* (ox) → the sound /ʔ/
- The sign for *beth* (house) → the sound /b/
- The sign for *mem* (water) → the sound /m/

## Significance
Proto-Sinaitic is the **common ancestor of nearly all alphabets** used in the world today. Through Phoenician, it gave rise to:
- The Greek, Latin and Cyrillic alphabets (Europe)
- The Arabic and Hebrew abjads (Middle East)
- The Brahmi family (South and Southeast Asia)
- Indirectly, the Hangul and other East Asian scripts
