# Arachnids (*Arachnida*)

## Overview
Arachnids are arthropods with **eight legs** and a body divided into two regions: the **cephalothorax** (prosoma) and the **abdomen** (opisthosoma). Around 100,000 species have been described.

## Major orders
| Order | Examples | Species |
|---|---|---|
| Araneae | Spiders | ~48,000 |
| Acari | Mites, ticks | ~55,000 |
| Scorpiones | Scorpions | ~2,500 |
| Opiliones | Harvestmen | ~6,600 |
| Pseudoscorpiones | Pseudoscorpions | ~3,400 |

## Key differences from insects
No antennae, no wings, 8 legs (vs 6), body in 2 sections (vs 3).

## Venom
Most spiders and all scorpions produce venom to immobilise prey. Only a small minority of species pose a genuine danger to humans.

## Ecological importance
Spiders alone are estimated to consume more insects annually than all birds and bats combined, providing essential regulation of insect populations in nearly every terrestrial ecosystem.
