# Emperor Scorpion (*Pandinus imperator*)

## Overview
The emperor scorpion is one of the largest scorpion species in the world, reaching up to **20 cm** in length. It inhabits the tropical rainforests of West Africa (Ghana, Nigeria, Cameroon, Togo and neighbouring countries).

## Morphology
- Glossy black exoskeleton with large, well-developed pincers (chelicerae and pedipalps).
- Eight legs, two claws, a segmented tail ending in a venomous stinger (telson).

## Venom
Despite its intimidating size, the emperor scorpion's venom is **relatively mild** to humans. It relies primarily on its powerful pincers to subdue prey, resorting to the stinger mainly as a last defence.

## Diet
Insects, millipedes and small vertebrates. Hunts at night, detecting prey through fine sensory hairs (trichobothria) that pick up air and substrate vibrations.

## Social behaviour
Unusually among scorpions, emperor scorpions can live in **semi-social groups**, sharing communal burrows and tolerating close proximity.

## Conservation status
Listed on **CITES Appendix II** (regulated trade). High demand in the exotic pet trade has led to overharvesting in parts of its range.
