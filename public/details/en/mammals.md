# Mammals

## Overview
Mammals (*Mammalia*) are a class of vertebrates defined by three unique features: the presence of **hair**, the production of **milk** by mammary glands, and three **middle-ear bones** (malleus, incus, stapes).

## Thermoregulation
Mammals are endothermic ("warm-blooded"): they maintain a stable core body temperature regardless of their environment, enabling them to colonise extreme habitats (polar regions, deserts, deep ocean).

## Diversity
With ~6,500 species, mammals occupy an extraordinary range of niches:
- **Monotremes**: platypus, echidna (egg-laying)
- **Marsupials**: kangaroo, koala (development in a pouch)
- **Placentals**: 94% of species, foetal development in the uterus

## Reproduction
Most give birth to live young that are nursed on milk for a period ranging from a few weeks to several years.

## Conservation
Roughly 26% of species are threatened according to the IUCN, primarily due to habitat destruction, hunting and climate change.
