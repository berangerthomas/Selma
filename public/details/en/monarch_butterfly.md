# Monarch Butterfly (*Danaus plexippus*)

## Overview
The monarch butterfly is famous for one of the most spectacular animal migrations on Earth: up to **4,500 km** between breeding grounds in Canada and the northern United States and overwintering sites in the oyamel fir forests of Michoacán, Mexico.

## Migration cycle
- **Autumn**: southward migration (up to 150 km per day), guided by the sun's position and a circadian clock.
- **Winter**: monarchs cluster in dense colonies on trees in Mexico.
- **Spring**: three to four successive generations gradually move northward.

## Toxicity and warning colouration
Caterpillars feed exclusively on **milkweed** (*Asclepias*), sequestering cardiac glycosides that are toxic to most predators. The vivid orange-and-black adult wing pattern is classic **aposematic colouration**.

## Decline
The overwintering population has collapsed from an estimated ~1 billion individuals in 1996 to **fewer than 100 million** today, driven by herbicide-induced milkweed loss, deforestation in Mexico and climate disruption.

## Conservation status
Listed as **Endangered** (IUCN, 2022). Milkweed restoration programmes and protection of Mexican wintering forests are priority conservation actions.
