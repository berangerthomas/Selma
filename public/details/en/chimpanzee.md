# Common Chimpanzee (*Pan troglodytes*)

## Overview
The chimpanzee shares approximately **98.7%** of its DNA with humans. Four subspecies are recognised, all found across equatorial and tropical Africa.

## Behaviour and intelligence
- Uses tools: sticks to extract termites, rocks to crack nuts
- Maintains local cultures transmitted by social learning
- Communicates via gestures, facial expressions and vocalisations
- Recognises its own reflection in a mirror (self-awareness)

## Social life
Lives in fission-fusion communities of 20 to 150 individuals with a complex dominance hierarchy. Males form coalitions and engage in cooperative hunts.

## Diet
Omnivorous: fruit, leaves, insects and occasionally small mammals (cooperative hunting of colobus monkeys).

## Conservation status
**Endangered** (IUCN). Fewer than one million individuals estimated. Threats: deforestation, bushmeat hunting, infectious diseases.
