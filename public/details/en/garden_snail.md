# Garden Snail (*Cornu aspersum*)

## Overview
The garden snail (formerly *Helix aspersa*) is the most familiar terrestrial gastropod in Western Europe. Introduced to all inhabited continents, it is considered invasive outside its native range.

## Morphology
- Brown spiralled shell, 3–4.5 cm in diameter
- Soft, greyish body; two pairs of tentacles: the upper pair bears the eyes, the lower pair provides touch and smell

## Locomotion
Glides on a muscular foot using a self-secreted mucus layer (leaving a silver trail). Maximum speed: approximately 50 m/h.

## Diet
Herbivorous: decaying or living plant matter, fungi, and occasionally carrion. Can be a significant garden pest.

## Biology
- **Hermaphrodite**: possesses both male and female reproductive organs; cross-fertilisation is the norm.
- **Aestivation** in dry periods, **hibernation** in winter (sealed with a calcareous mucus plug).

## Gastronomy
Consumed since prehistoric times. The escargot (particularly Burgundy snail and petit-gris) is a celebrated dish in French and Spanish cuisine.
