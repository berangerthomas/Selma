# Apple

## Overview
The apple (Malus domestica) is a pomaceous fruit widely cultivated in temperate regions. Valued for its crisp texture and wide range of flavors, it is consumed fresh and processed worldwide.

## Botanical characteristics
Apples grow on deciduous trees with white to pink blossoms. Fruit size, color and sweetness vary greatly by cultivar.

## Varieties
Common varieties include Gala, Fuji, Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, and Golden Delicious — each suited to different uses (fresh eating, baking, cider).

## Uses
- Fresh eating
- Cooking: pies, tarts, sauces, compotes
- Beverages: cider, juice
- Processing: drying, preserves, chutneys

## Cultivation
Prefers well-drained soils and a temperate climate with sufficient chill hours. Requires pruning, pest management and insect pollinators; typically propagated by grafting.

## Nutrition
Good source of dietary fiber (pectin), vitamin C and various antioxidants; low in calories.

## Storage & tips
Store apples cool and humid (refrigerator crisper) to prolong freshness; keep varieties separate from ethylene-sensitive produce when possible.
