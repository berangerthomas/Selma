# Syriac Script

## Overview
Syriac is a dialect of Middle Aramaic that became the literary and liturgical language of many Eastern Christian communities. Its script, derived from Aramaic, was a **co-parent of Arabic** (alongside Nabataean) and is also thought to have influenced the Armenian alphabet.

## Three Forms
The Syriac script has three distinct calligraphic styles:

| Style | Name | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Esṭrangelā** | "Round script" | Classical form, manuscripts |
| **Serṭo** (Serto) | Western Syriac | Syriac Orthodox Church |
| **Maḏnḥāyā** | Eastern Syriac | Church of the East (Assyrian) |

## Christian Heritage
Syriac was the language of the early Eastern church and produced a vast literature:
- Translations of the Bible (the **Peshitta**)
- The works of **Ephrem the Syrian** (4th c.) — one of the most prolific Christian writers
- Early theological, philosophical and scientific texts that later passed into Arabic and thence into medieval Europe

## Living Script
Syriac remains in liturgical use today in:
- The Syriac Orthodox Church
- The Maronite Church
- The Church of the East (Assyrian Church)
- The Chaldean Catholic Church

It is also being revived as a spoken literary language by Assyrian communities.
