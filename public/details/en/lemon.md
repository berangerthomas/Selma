# Lemon

## Overview
The lemon (Citrus limon) is a bright yellow citrus fruit prized for its acidic juice and fragrant zest. It is widely cultivated in warm climates.

## Botanical characteristics
Lemons grow on small evergreen trees with glossy leaves and white fragrant flowers. Fruits are typically 4–7 cm long with a thick rind and pulpy interior.

## Uses
- Culinary: juice, zest, marmalades, dressings, beverages.
- Household: natural cleaner, de-greaser, deodorizer.
- Health & cosmetics: vitamin C source, flavoring, aromatherapy.

## Cultivation
Prefers subtropical to Mediterranean climates, well-drained soil and full sun. Propagated by grafting or cuttings; sensitive to frost.

## Nutrition
Low in calories and rich in vitamin C, flavonoids, and citric acid.

## Storage & tips
Store refrigerated to extend freshness; use zest before juice for concentrated flavor. Avoid prolonged skin exposure to juice.
