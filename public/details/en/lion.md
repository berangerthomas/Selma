# Lion (*Panthera leo*)

![Photo of a lion](/assets/images/Lion_waiting_in_Namibia.jpg)
*Photo credit: Kevin Pluck (CC BY 2.0)*

## Overview
The lion is the largest cat in Africa and the only felid to live in social groups called **prides**. It inhabits savannahs, grasslands and open woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa, with a small relict population in the Gir Forest of India.

## Physical characteristics
- Weight: 120–250 kg (male), 120–180 kg (female)
- Length: up to 3.3 m including tail
- The male's mane, a sign of maturity and dominance, ranges from blond to black.

## Social behaviour
Prides typically contain 3 to 30 individuals. Females cooperate to hunt and raise cubs. Males defend the territory against rival predators.

## Diet
An opportunistic predator targeting wildebeest, zebras, buffalo and impala. Lionesses perform ~80% of hunts, typically at dusk or during the night.

## Conservation status
**Vulnerable** (IUCN). Populations have declined by 40% over three generations. Threats include habitat loss, conflict with livestock farming and poaching.
