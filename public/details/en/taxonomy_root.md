# Animal Kingdom

## General overview
The Animal Kingdom (*Animalia*) encompasses all multicellular, heterotrophic eukaryotic organisms capable of voluntary movement. With an estimated **8.7 million species** — only ~1.5 million formally described — it is one of the largest subdivisions of life.

## Position in the tree of life
Animals belong to the domain Eukaryota. They differ from plants in their inability to photosynthesize, and from fungi in their mode of internal digestion.

## Major subdivisions
- **Vertebrates**: fish, amphibians, reptiles, birds, mammals — all bearing a vertebral column.
- **Invertebrates**: arthropods, mollusks, annelids, echinoderms, cnidarians… — lacking an internal bony skeleton.

## Evolutionary origins
The earliest multicellular animals appeared roughly **600 million years ago**. The Cambrian explosion (~540 Ma) saw the rapid emergence of virtually all the major body plans known today.

## Ecological roles
Animals play fundamental roles as pollinators, decomposers, apex predators, seed dispersers and ecosystem engineers, shaping the environments they inhabit.
