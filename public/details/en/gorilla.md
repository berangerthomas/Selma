# Gorilla (*Gorilla* spp.)

## Overview
The gorilla is the largest living primate. Two species exist: the Western gorilla (*G. gorilla*) and the Eastern gorilla (*G. beringei*), each divided into subspecies.

## Morphology
- Adult male (silverback): 140–200 kg, up to 1.8 m standing
- Limited brachiating; predominantly knuckle-walking on the ground

## Behaviour
Diurnal, generally calm and peaceful outside male confrontations. A family group is led by a dominant silverback male.

## Diet
Predominantly herbivorous: leaves, stems, fruit and roots. Consumes up to 30 kg of vegetation daily.

## Communication
Vocalisations (grunts, roars) and postural signals. The silverback chest-beats to signal strength and authority.

## Conservation status
**Critically Endangered** (mountain gorilla subspecies: only ~1,000 individuals remaining). Deforestation, poaching and disease are the primary threats.
