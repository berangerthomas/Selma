# Sogdian Script

## Overview
The Sogdian script was the writing system of the **Sogdians**, an Iranian merchant people based in Central Asia (modern Uzbekistan and Tajikistan). They were the dominant traders of the **Silk Road** from roughly 200 to 900 CE.

## Origins
Sogdian derived from the **Aramaic** script, adapted for the Iranian language of Sogd. Like Aramaic, it was an abjad written right to left.

## Silk Road Network
Sogdian merchants established a commercial network stretching from the Mediterranean to China. Sogdian letters found in dunhuang (dated c. 313–314 CE) are among the oldest known private letters in any language — written by stranded merchants in China to their families in Samarkand.

## Importance as Intermediary
The Sogdian script is a pivotal intermediary in writing history. It gave rise to:
- **Old Uyghur script** (adapted by the Turkic Uyghurs, 8th c.)
- Which gave rise to **Old Mongolian script** (used for Mongolian to this day)
- Which influenced **Manchu script** and **Buryat script**

## Decline
As Islam spread into Central Asia from the 8th century onward, Arabic script displaced Sogdian. The script became extinct around the 10th–11th century.
