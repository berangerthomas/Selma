# The Phoenician Alphabet and Its Descendants

## Abstract
The Phoenician alphabet, developed on the Levantine coast around 1050 BCE, is the most consequential writing innovation after the initial invention of writing itself. This article traces how its 22-consonant abjad gave rise to the vast majority of writing systems used in the modern world.

---

## 1. Historical Context

The Phoenicians were a Semitic-speaking seafaring people who established city-states at Byblos, Tyre, Sidon and Carthage. Their extensive Mediterranean trade network — stretching from the Levant to Spain — made their alphabet an ideal vehicle for commercial record-keeping, and incidentally spread it across the ancient world.

The alphabet they used was not their invention: it descended from **Proto-Sinaitic** (c. 1850–1400 BCE), itself adapted from Egyptian hieroglyphs by Semitic-speaking workers in the Sinai. The Phoenician contribution was **standardisation and dissemination**.

---

## 2. The Acrophonic Principle

Each of the 22 letters was named after an object whose Semitic name began with that letter's sound:

- **ʾAleph** (𐤀) — *alp*, "ox" → sound /ʔ/
- **Beth** (𐤁) — *bayt*, "house" → sound /b/
- **Gimel** (𐤂) — *gaml*, "camel" → sound /g/
- **Daleth** (𐤃) — *dalt*, "door" → sound /d/

The letter shapes were originally pictographic representations of these objects, increasingly abstracted over time.

---

## 3. Major Descendant Branches

### 3.1 The Greek Branch
Around 800 BCE, Greek traders adapted the Phoenician alphabet, making a decisive modification: repurposing consonant letters for sounds not present in Greek as **vowel signs**. This created the first true alphabet. Greek then gave rise to:
- **Etruscan** → **Latin** → virtually all European scripts (Roman, modern)
- **Glagolitic** → **Cyrillic** (Slavic world)
- **Coptic** (with Demotic, for Egyptian)
- **Armenian** (with Syriac)

### 3.2 The Aramaic Branch
Aramaic, adopted as the lingua franca of the Persian Empire, carried an evolved form of Phoenician across Asia:
- **Hebrew** (square script — also from Phoenician directly)
- **Nabataean** → **Arabic**
- **Syriac** → contributed to **Arabic** and **Armenian**
- **Sogdian** → **Uyghur** → **Mongolian**
- **Brahmi** → 100+ South and Southeast Asian scripts

### 3.3 The South Arabian Branch
A parallel development in the Arabian Peninsula produced the **South Arabian** script, carried across the Red Sea to become **Ge'ez** (Ethiopic) — today used for Amharic and Tigrinya.

## 4. Statistics

| Descendant family | Approximate speakers today |
|---|---|
| Latin-derived scripts | ~3 billion |
| Arabic script | ~1.8 billion |
| Brahmi-derived scripts | ~1.5 billion |
| Cyrillic | ~250 million |
| Hebrew | ~10 million |

**Total: over 6 billion people write with alphabets traceable to Phoenician.**
