# Guide to Egyptian Hieroglyphs

## Reading Hieroglyphs: Where to Start

Egyptian hieroglyphs can be written left-to-right, right-to-left, or top-to-bottom. The **direction of reading** is determined by which way the animal and human figures face: read *toward* them.

## The Three Sign Types

### 1. Logograms (Word Signs)
A single sign represents an entire word.

| Sign | Transliteration | Meaning |
|:---:|---|---|
| 𓂀 | jr | eye |
| 𓇳 | rꜥ | sun / Ra |
| 𓈖 | n | water |
| 𓌀 | ḥtp | offering / peace |

### 2. Phonograms (Sound Signs)
Signs represent one, two or three consonants (no vowels).

| Sign | Consonants | Example word |
|:---:|---|---|
| 𓄿 | ꜣ (glottal) | ꜣ-bird |
| 𓇋 | j | |
| 𓂝 | ꜥ (ayin) | |
| 𓅱 | w | |

### 3. Determinatives (Semantic Classifiers)
Silent signs placed at the end of a word to indicate its category.

| Sign | Category |
|:---:|---|
| 𓀀 | Man, male person |
| 𓁐 | Woman, female person |
| 𓂉 | Body parts |
| 𓇍 | Plants, vegetation |
| 𓇯 | Sky, time, celestial |

## Famous Hieroglyphic Texts

1. **Pyramid Texts** (c. 2400–2300 BCE) — oldest known religious texts; carved inside Old Kingdom pyramids
2. **Coffin Texts** (c. 2100–1800 BCE) — spells painted inside Middle Kingdom coffins
3. **Book of the Dead** (c. 1550 BCE–50 CE) — illustrated papyrus scrolls guiding the deceased through the afterlife
4. **Rosetta Stone** (196 BCE) — trilingual priestly decree; key to decipherment
